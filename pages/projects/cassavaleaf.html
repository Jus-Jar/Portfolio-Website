Project Overview

Develop an offline, mobile-friendly multi-class classification model to accurately identify viral diseases affecting cassava plants through images of their leaves. As a Kaggle competition attempt, this solution aims to empower smallholder farmers in Sub-Saharan Africa with a cost-effective, accessible, and efficient diagnostic tool to improve crop yield and food security.

The model aims to differentiate viral diseases such as Cassava Bacterial Blight (CBB), Cassava Brown Streak Disease (CBSD), Cassava Green Mottle (CGM), and Cassava Mosaic Disease (CMD) from healthy leaves based on images provided to the model.

Model Design and Training

The Ensemble Learning strategy was utilized for the Cassava Leaf Disease Classification task. This technique aims to boost model performance by leveraging the diversity among different models, resulting in combined outputs that are more accurate, robust, and less prone to overfitting compared to individual models. Our specific approach involves the Model Averaging Ensemble Learning method, which trains multiple models with varying architectures, hyperparameters, and random initializations on the same dataset. The outputs from each model are then averaged, and the class with the highest average probability is chosen as the predicted class.
